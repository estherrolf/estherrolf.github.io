Esther Rolf

I'm a researcher studying statistical machine learning. My research interests span geospatial machine learning, responsible and fair algorithm design and use, and the influence of data acquisition and representivity on the efficacy and applicability of machine learning systems.

I’m a postdoctoral fellow at Harvard with the Harvard Data Science Initiative (HDSI) and Center for Research on Computation and Society (CRCS).

In fall of 2024 I’ll join CU Boulder as an assistant professor of Computer Science!

Research

I study statistical and geospatial machine learning. My research blends methodological and applied techniques to study and design machine learning algorithms and systems with an emphasis on usability, data-efficiency and fairness. Specific projects span developing algorithms and infrastructure for reliable environmental monitoring using machine learning and understanding the role and multifaceted nature representativity in data for training fair and effective machine learning systems.

For a full list of papers please see my google scholar page.
